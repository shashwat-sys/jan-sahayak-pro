**For:** PLVs, frontline workers, young activists, and community leaders  
**Resource persons:** Nawaz, Nagmani, Tausif

## How to use this workbook

- Bring this workbook into every session.
- Write in it during discussions and activities.
- Use it after the training for real cases.
- Keep copies of complaints, applications, and representations with this workbook if possible.

## Training promise

By the end of the day, you should be able to:

- understand basic legal action steps in community cases
- write a stronger complaint or FIR-style application
- write a representation to higher authorities
- deal with police in a safe and strategic way
- link a person to welfare and legal aid systems
- decide when to escalate and when to refer

## Core message

**First refusal is not the end of the road.**

Field method:

**Listen -> Record -> Classify -> Draft -> Submit -> Follow up -> Escalate -> Refer**

### FIR basics to remember

- FIR teaching for new complaints should be based on `Section 173 of the BNSS, 2023`
- information may be oral, written, or electronic
- information should be recorded properly
- the informant or victim should receive a free copy
- refusal should be documented and escalated

### Police engagement checklist

Before going:

- carry copies, not only originals
- carry written facts
- do not go alone in a high-risk case
- identify urgent safety needs

At the police station:

- state facts clearly
- stay calm and firm
- ask for recording of the information
- note the name, rank, date, time, and response
- ask for copy or acknowledgment if available

After refusal or delay:

- preserve your written complaint
- record what happened
- identify the next authority
- take legal aid support where needed
